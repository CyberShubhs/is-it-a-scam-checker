&quot;$45 an hour, work from home, no experience required.&quot; If it sounds too good to be true, it almost always is. The FTC&apos;s consumer-protection data shows job and business opportunity scams cost US victims over $367 million in 2024, with the bulk concentrated in remote-work and task-based fake jobs.

Here is what these offers usually are — and the one-minute test that catches almost all of them.

## The three remote-job scam variants

### 1. Task / swiping scams

You are added to a Telegram or WhatsApp group offering &quot;tasks&quot; — rating products, watching videos, liking posts. Early micro-payouts build trust. Then you are asked to &quot;top up&quot; your account to unlock higher-paying tasks. The top-up is gone. Top-ups continue until you stop paying.

### 2. Fake trading-platform jobs

The job is described as customer support or junior trading. The platform is a fake exchange. Your &quot;commissions&quot; are displayed numbers in a dashboard. When you try to withdraw, you are blocked behind a &quot;tax&quot; or &quot;verification fee&quot;.

### 3. Onboarding identity theft

The offer is fake but generous. You complete &quot;new-hire paperwork&quot; — passport scan, tax ID, bank details. Nothing happens. Your identity is sold.

## The one-minute test

Real entry-level remote jobs that pay $45/hour are exceptionally rare. Real ones almost never:

- Come via an unsolicited WhatsApp or Telegram DM.
- Skip the interview.
- Require any payment from you.
- Use a Gmail / Outlook / ProtonMail address for &quot;HR&quot;.
- Ask for ID before a signed contract.

If three or more of those are present, the offer is a scam. Stop replying, do not share documents, and report it.

## Red flags in &quot;too good to be true&quot; remote roles

- 🚩 Unsolicited contact about a role you didn&apos;t apply for.
- 🚩 The recruiter wants to move you to WhatsApp or Telegram immediately.
- 🚩 You are added to a paid &quot;training&quot; group or asked to install an app to start tasks.
- 🚩 The job description is vague — &quot;data entry&quot;, &quot;reviewer&quot;, &quot;product rater&quot;, &quot;assistant&quot;.
- 🚩 Salary is two to four times market rate for the work described.
- 🚩 You can &quot;start immediately&quot; with no interview.

## What real remote roles look like

Even at experienced rates, real remote roles have a slow, traceable pipeline:

- A job listing on the company&apos;s own careers page.
- An applicant tracking system that confirms receipt of your CV.
- A scheduled video interview with a real employee whose LinkedIn you can verify.
- A formal offer letter on company letterhead, signed by a named hiring manager.
- Onboarding paperwork sent through a known HR system (Workday, BambooHR, Rippling, Deel), not as Word docs from Gmail.

## Verify before you reply

1. Search the company on its real domain and on Google.
2. Find the company on Companies House (UK), ABN Lookup (AU), or your local register.
3. Find at least three real employees on LinkedIn.
4. Run the recruiter messages through our [free scam checker](/check).
5. If the company seems real, contact them on the phone number listed on their actual website — not the recruiter&apos;s.

## If you already engaged

- Sent any money? Call your bank&apos;s fraud line now.
- Shared ID documents? Contact IDCARE (Australia/NZ on `1800 595 160`), [IdentityTheft.gov](https://www.identitytheft.gov/) (US), or Action Fraud (UK).
- Installed an app for &quot;tasks&quot;? Uninstall it, change your bank passwords, run a security scan.
- Walk through the [damage-control checklist](/have-i-been-scammed).
