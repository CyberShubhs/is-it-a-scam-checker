# Scam Checker — Guest Post & Digital PR Plan

Date: 2026-05-23

This plan is the editorial-only counterpart to the on-site SEO/AEO work. Goal: earn legitimate citations and referring links by being genuinely useful to publishers and their audiences — never by paying for placement or trading favours.

---

## 1. Goal and positioning

**Goal.** Build topical authority for Scam Checker as a free, privacy-first scam-detection resource so that journalists, consumer-protection editors, and AI summarisation systems cite the canonical URL when explaining "how to check if X is a scam" or "what to do if I've been scammed."

**Positioning.** Scam Checker is the practical, action-oriented layer between a victim's first moment of doubt and a government reporting body. Not a news site. Not a paid advisory. Not a security vendor. The pitch we lead with:

- Free, no sign-up, runs in the browser, nothing stored.
- Built by someone with a cybersecurity background — credible but not a corporate vendor.
- Covers fraud patterns globally with country-specific reporting paths.
- Maintains an interactive damage-control checklist for people who think they've already been scammed.

**Authority anchors that earn coverage:**

- The interactive `/have-i-been-scammed` damage-control flow.
- Honest tool boundaries (e.g. we don't run live carrier lookup; we don't recover crypto).
- Country-specific reporting directory.
- Open methodology — the site's code is on GitHub.

---

## 2. What kinds of sites to target

Editorial-first targets where Scam Checker content would genuinely fit a reader brief:

- **Cybersecurity awareness blogs** with a consumer slant (e.g. KrebsOnSecurity-style independents, Bleeping Computer reader columns, national CERT blogs).
- **Consumer protection blogs** and complaint-resolution sites (Choice AU, Which? UK, Consumer Reports US contributor desks).
- **Small-business associations** publishing fraud-awareness content (chambers of commerce, regional SBDC blogs).
- **University career centers** and student-aid offices publishing job-scam guidance.
- **Senior-safety organisations** (AARP-style, regional senior cyber-safety programs).
- **Parenting/family safety blogs** with a "kids and money" or "elder safety" desk.
- **Fintech and personal-finance education sites** (Money Smart AU, Money Helper UK, NerdWallet/Lifehacker columns).
- **Crypto safety communities** (Reddit r/scams adjacent newsletters; sub-Stack writers focused on wallet hygiene).
- **Local consumer-safety resources** in AU, UK, US, Canada, India — community newspapers, council pages, state police outreach desks.
- **Journalists covering scams, fraud, cybercrime, and consumer protection** — pitch as a source, not a guest writer.

---

## 3. What kinds of sites to avoid

Avoid anything that would invite a Google manual action or that the team doesn't actually believe in:

- Paid guest-post farms (`linkbuilder123.io`-style brokerages).
- Sites selling "dofollow placements" or "DA 50+ guaranteed".
- Link-exchange schemes (you-link-me / I-link-you reciprocity).
- Private blog networks (PBNs), expired-domain rebuilds, and content-farm directories.
- Mass-submission directories (no editorial review).
- Sites where 90%+ of posts are guest contributions.
- Sites where the topic match is forced (e.g. a wedding planning blog).
- Sites that demand the article be entirely keyword-stuffed.
- Sites that hide outbound links behind JavaScript or `noindex` (no SEO value, but also unethical).
- Any site that asks for the keyword to be repeated 5+ times in the body.

## 6. Outreach qualification checklist

Before pitching, every target site must pass this checklist. If any item is "no", do not pitch.

- [ ] Real editor name and contact, ideally a public bio.
- [ ] Active publishing cadence in the last 90 days.
- [ ] At least one prior post that is genuinely well-written, not AI-spun.
- [ ] No visible "sponsored post" rate card or "buy a guest post" page.
- [ ] Site is indexed in Google for its own brand search.
- [ ] Outbound links are real HTML `<a>` elements (not redirect-cloaked).
- [ ] The audience genuinely overlaps with one of the listed scam topics.
- [ ] The proposed angle is something the site has not already published in the last 12 months.
- [ ] If the site is a guest-only platform, skip it.

## 8. Link policy

- **Editorial links** (the publisher chose to link us in the body of a piece they paid no one to write) — always fine.
- **Contributed posts** — fine as long as the publisher's editorial standards apply and the post does not exist primarily as a link vehicle. Maximum one outbound link to scamchecker.app per article. Prefer the most specific deep link (e.g. the guide we're citing), not the homepage.
- **Paid placements / sponsored content / advertorials** — must carry `rel="sponsored"` (preferred) or `rel="nofollow"`. We never pay for follow links.
- **Author bio links** — fine, single link, `rel="author"` or unqualified is acceptable.
- **Anchor text** — natural language only. Prefer branded ("Scam Checker"), URL anchors ("scamchecker.app/guides/job-scams"), and descriptive natural anchors ("Scam Checker's job scam guide"). Do not request exact-match SEO anchors like "free scam checker" repeatedly.
- **Forbidden** — link exchanges, PBNs, mass guest-post farms, low-quality directories, comment-section link drops, expired-domain redirects, link wheels.
- **Disclosure** — if a partner discloses they were paid by us (which we should not be doing), they must mark links `sponsored`. If a partner links because they found the resource useful, no disclosure is required.

## 9. Monthly execution plan

A sustainable, low-volume cadence. Quality > quantity.

| Week 2 | Pitch round (Template A) | 5–8 personalised pitches |

| Week 3 | Draft accepted articles | 1–3 drafts at 900–1,200 words |
| Week 3 | Resource-mention follow-ups (Template C) | 5 suggestions |
| Week 4 | Publication & confirmation | 1–3 live placements |
| Week 4 | Embed offers (Template D) | 3–5 publishers |
| Week 4 | Retro | Update the qualification checklist and pitch templates based on response rate |

Target outcome per quarter: 3–6 editorial placements + 3–5 cited mentions + 1 new original-research asset.

## 10. Metrics to track

Tracked monthly. Snapshots stored under `SEO/reports/`.

**Acquisition / authority**
- Referring domains (count and quality cohort).
- New backlinks from sites passing the §6 qualification (manual review of top 10/month).
- Branded search impressions in Google Search Console for `scam checker`, `scamchecker.app`, `scam checker app`.
- Brand mentions (linked + unlinked) — track via manual Google + Bing searches monthly.

**Conversion / engagement**
- `/check` and `/have-i-been-scammed` sessions from referral traffic.
- Time on page and scroll depth for pages cited by guest posts.
- `check_submitted` GA4 events sourced from referral channels.

**AI visibility (manual, monthly)**
- Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude these three prompts and record whether Scam Checker is cited or referenced:
  - "What is Scam Checker?"
  - "How can I check if a job offer is a scam?"
  - "What should I do if I clicked a scam link?"
- Record the canonical URL each model cites, if any.

**Pipeline hygiene**
- Pitch-to-publication rate (target ≥ 15%).
- Outreach response rate (target ≥ 30%).
- Number of pitches that resulted in "no, but happy to keep you on file" — these are signal, not failure.

**Anti-patterns to flag**
- Any inbound link from a domain matching the avoid list in §3.
- Anchor-text spikes (a single anchor used > 30% of total). If detected, audit and disavow if necessary.
- Any reciprocal-link request that came from a partner we pitched.
