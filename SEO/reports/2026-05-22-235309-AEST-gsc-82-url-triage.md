## Methodology

Each URL was classified by route type, inspected for word count / source quality / duplicate signal, and assigned one of five actions:

- **keep** — page is fine; expected to be re-evaluated by Google after recrawl.
- **improve** — page is real but thin or weakly internal-linked; needs additional content or links (not all blocking; "monitor" overlaps).
- **consolidate** — duplicate of another page; redirect to canonical.
- **redirect** — thin / generic / unsourced; redirect to closest stronger page; remove the underlying MDX so it leaves the sitemap.
- **monitor** — strong page that Google has crawled but not yet indexed; no action other than waiting and watching impressions.

Word counts are body-only (`wc -w`). The bar of 500 words is the cut-off used in `scripts/check-blog-quality.mjs`.
